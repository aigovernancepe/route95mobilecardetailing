## H2: Why Does Leather Need Professional Cleaning?

Leather absorbs oils and contaminants that home cleaning products cannot safely remove without risking damage to the material.

Every time you drive, your body transfers oils and products to your leather seats. Sunscreen is especially damaging. Lotions, hand creams, and natural skin oils all accumulate on leather surfaces. In Fort Lauderdale's climate, you're applying sunscreen almost daily—and much of it ends up on your seats.

These contaminants don't just sit on the surface. They penetrate into the leather, breaking down the protective finish and eventually the leather itself. You'll notice the driver's seat showing wear long before passenger seats because that's where oils concentrate most.

Professional cleaning uses pH-balanced products designed specifically for automotive leather. We lift these embedded contaminants safely without stripping the leather's natural properties or factory finish.

## H2: Our Leather Cleaning Process

**Step 1: Inspection**
We examine all leather surfaces to identify heavy soil areas, existing damage, and material type.

**Step 2: Vacuuming**
Loose dirt and debris gets removed first to prevent grinding it into the leather during cleaning.

**Step 3: Cleaning Solution**
We apply Chemical Guys leather cleaner using soft brushes that agitate dirt without scratching.

**Step 4: Wiping**
Clean microfiber towels lift away the cleaner along with dissolved oils and grime.

**Step 5: Drying**
Leather air dries briefly before any conditioning treatment is applied.

## H2: Should I Condition Leather After Cleaning?

Leather conditioning after cleaning restores moisture and creates protection against future damage from Florida sun and humidity.

Cleaning removes oils—both the bad ones from your skin and the good ones that keep leather supple. Conditioning replaces that moisture and adds a protective barrier.

We strongly recommend combining leather cleaning with leather conditioning, especially for vehicles driven daily in Fort Lauderdale. The Florida sun dries out leather faster than almost anywhere else in the country.
