## H2: Why Is Pet Hair So Hard to Remove?

Pet hair has a barbed structure that grips fabric fibers. Static electricity makes it cling to surfaces. Regular vacuums don't have the suction or attachments to pull embedded hair free.

Florida's dog-friendly lifestyle means lots of pets in cars. We have year-round park weather, dog-friendly beaches, and outdoor cafes that welcome pets. All those trips mean hair buildup.

Pet hair causes several problems beyond just looking messy:

- **Allergies** — Hair and dander trigger reactions in sensitive passengers
- **Odor** — Hair holds body oils and outdoor smells that build up over time
- **AC contamination** — Loose hair gets pulled into vents and circulates through your cabin
- **Resale impact** — Pet hair is one of the first things buyers notice when looking at a used car

Signs you need professional pet hair removal:
- Visible hair on seats despite vacuuming
- Hair floating in the air when you sit down
- Passengers with allergies react in your car
- Hair stuck in seat crevices and door panels
- You're preparing to sell the vehicle
