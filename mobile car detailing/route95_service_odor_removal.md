## H2: Why Do Cars Develop Bad Odors in Fort Lauderdale?

Florida's heat and humidity accelerate odor development. Food spoils faster, moisture breeds mold, and bacteria thrive in warm, damp conditions.

Common odor sources in Fort Lauderdale vehicles:

- **Mildew** — Moisture from rain, wet swimsuits, or humidity gets trapped and grows mold. That musty smell is mildew spores.
- **Food** — Forgotten items spoil quickly in Florida heat. Even crumbs can attract moisture and start smelling.
- **Spills** — Drinks that soak into carpet or seats feed bacteria as they break down.
- **Pet accidents** — Urine soaks deep into carpet padding and requires enzymatic treatment to fully eliminate.
- **AC system** — Mold grows in your air conditioning evaporator, blowing musty air every time you run the AC.
- **Body odor** — Sweat absorbed by seats over time creates a sour smell.

Signs you need professional odor removal:
- Bad smell returns after cleaning or air fresheners
- You can't identify where the smell is coming from
- The odor gets worse on hot days
- Your AC blows musty-smelling air
- Passengers comment on the smell
